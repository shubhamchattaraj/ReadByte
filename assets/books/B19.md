The elevator always hesitated before the fourth floor.

Ethan Carter noticed it on his first evening in Ashcroft Apartments...
... Apartment numbers read 401, 402, 403, then 405. The brass plate
where 404 should have been looked older than the others, as if something
had once been screwed into the wall and carefully removed.

The old landlord smiled whenever Ethan asked about it.

"There has never been a 404."

The answer should have ended the conversation, but every night, just
after midnight, a folded note appeared beneath Ethan's door.

**Don't knock on 404.**

At first Ethan assumed it was a prank. Then he found footprints in the
dusty hallway leading toward a section of wall that seemed just slightly
darker than the rest.

He began asking neighbors.

Every answer was identical.

"There is no Apartment 404."

None of them sounded like they believed their own words.

Days turned into weeks. Strange noises echoed through the ventilation
system. Lights flickered only on the fourth floor. Security footage
skipped exactly twenty-three seconds whenever Ethan approached the
missing apartment.

His dreams grew vivid. In them, someone knocked three times from the
other side of a door that did not exist.

One rainy night the note changed.

**Someone already did.**

...

Ethan eventually found the hidden doorway behind peeling wallpaper in an
abandoned service corridor.

The apartment inside looked impossibly normal.

Fresh coffee.

Warm lights.

Family photographs.

Every picture contained Ethan.

An elderly woman sat in a rocking chair.

"You finally came home," she whispered.

She explained that Apartment 404 was where forgotten people waited after
the world erased them. Every tenant slowly disappeared from history
until no one remembered they had ever existed.

The apartment survived by convincing lonely people to enter willingly.

Ethan ran.

When he reached the street, dawn had broken.

The building stood exactly as before.

Police searched every floor.

No hidden apartment existed.

The landlord insisted Ethan had imagined everything.

At home, Ethan opened his wallet to prove his identity.

His driver's license was gone.

Only a faded lease agreement remained.

Tenant: Ethan Carter

Apartment: 404

He looked up.

Across the street stood Ashcroft Apartments.

A single light glowed on the fourth floor.

For the first time, a brass number hung on the forgotten door.

404. 

Someone knocked from inside.

The next morning, Ethan's coworkers wondered why his desk had been empty
for weeks.

His parents insisted they had never had a son.

His bank account belonged to someone else.

His photographs faded.

His name disappeared from every document except the lease.

Months later, another tenant moved into Ashcroft Apartments.

The directory listed 401, 402, 403, and 405.

Beneath their door, just after midnight, a folded note appeared.

**Don't knock on 404.**
