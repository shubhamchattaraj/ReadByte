I draw the maps of streets that don't exist,
by candlelight, when everyone's asleep,
I chart the alleys hidden in the mist,
and mark the corners where the shadows keep.

My compass is a memory of dreams,
my ruler bent from nights I can't explain,
I trace the rivers, though they're only streams
of ink that fall like quiet, silver rain.

They ask me why I chart what no one sees,
why waste the ink on streets that can't be walked,
I tell them dreams deserve their own degrees
of distance, even if they're never talked.

So I'll keep drawing till the candle dies,
each line a road no waking foot has pressed,
the midnight cartographer never lies,
he only maps the places we love best.
