The moonlight came in hollow through the shutters,
a light that filled the room but touched no thing,
it fell across the floor in silver mutters,
too thin to warm, too soft to fully sing.

I've sat inside that hollow, quiet glowing,
and let it wrap me, weightless, cool, and pale,
the hollow moonlight doesn't ask for knowing
the reasons why the night feels this way, this frail.

They say the moon just borrows light for shining,
reflecting back a sun it cannot keep,
perhaps that's why its glow feels so resigning,
so hollow, soft, and perfect for the sleep.

So let the hollow moonlight keep on falling,
across the floor in shades of silver-gray,
some light exists for nothing but the calling
of quiet minds to rest until the day.
