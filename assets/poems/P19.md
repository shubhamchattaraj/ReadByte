The dust is settling on the wooden street,
The sun is dipping low behind the ridge.
The horses stomp with restless, rhythmic feet,
Beside the crossing of the dried-up bridge.
The shadows stretch like fingers long and lean,
Across the quiet, lonely desert scene.

The whiskey burns as smooth as polished steel,
Inside the dim and smoky, loud saloon.
The card games lose their sharp and desperate feel,
Beneath the rising of the desert moon.
A gambler deals his cards with steady hands,
While wind is whispering across the sands.

The sheriff waits with iron on his hip,
For trouble that is coming down the trail.
He bites his tongue and keeps a steady grip,
Against the winds that start to fiercely wail.
The code of law is written in the ground,
Where only silence ever can be found.
