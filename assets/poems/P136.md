The vellum's come undone along the edges,
its ancient binding loosening with age,
the words I wrote in youth, in careful pledges,
now drift in fragments, loose upon the page.

I've tried to stitch the vellum back together,
to hold the story tight the way it was,
but pages, like the seasons and the weather,
don't always keep the shape or keep the cause.

They say a book that's coming undone is broken,
that torn pages mean the story's lost,
but even scattered words are somehow spoken,
regardless of the binding, or the cost.

So let the vellum stay a bit undone,
I'll read the fragments in whatever order,
some stories aren't diminished, only spun
in looser shapes across a looser border.
