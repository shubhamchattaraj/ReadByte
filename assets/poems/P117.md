The wildflowers rose up against the pavement,
a quiet insurgency of root and stem,
they cracked the concrete with their slow enslavement
to sunlight, breaking every rule of them.

No one gave permission for their growing,
no city planner drew them on the map,
but still they came, their colors quietly showing
the borders where the wild refused to nap.

I've watched them win against the gray and solid,
one crack, one bloom, one stubborn green at a time,
the wildflower insurgency stays valid,
in every sidewalk breaking into rhyme.

So let the flowers keep on their rebellion,
against the concrete trying to keep them small,
some uprisings don't need a swelling battalion,
just roots, and light, and patience through it all.
