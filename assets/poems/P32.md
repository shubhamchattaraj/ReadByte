The waves are crashing on the rusted shore,
An iron tide that surges from the deep.
It brings the secrets of the sea once more,
To wake the ones who have been lost in sleep.
The salt is heavy on the winter wind,
Where all the ghosts of sailors have been pinned.

The ship is anchored to the rocky bed,
A skeleton against the rising tide.
It tells the stories of the long-since dead,
With nowhere left for any soul to hide.
The iron rusts beneath the rising foam,
A place that we no longer call our home.

The current pulls the wreckage to the sand,
A gift of sorrow from the ocean floor.
We watch it drift towards the hungry land,
And wonder what it was looking for.
The water is a mirror of the grey,
That steals the light of every passing day.

The tide is pulling back into the night,
And leaving everything to rot in peace.
There is no longer any sign of light,
For all the ways that human struggles cease.
The iron tide is resting in the deep,
Where all the secrets of the ocean sleep.
