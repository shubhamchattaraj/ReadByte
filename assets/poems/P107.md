I walked the meridian without my shoes on,
felt every degree pressed into my skin,
the line that splits the world was never news on
the maps I made of everywhere I've been.

Barefoot, I crossed from east to west by feeling,
the temperature of longitude and time,
each step a kind of quiet, humble kneeling,
before a line too thin to call a crime.

They say you need a compass for this walking,
a shoe, a map, a reason to depart,
but I have learned the meridian's just talking,
a thread that only matters to the heart.

So let me walk it barefoot, calm and steady,
I'll feel the line instead of reading maps,
some borders only open when you're ready
to cross them soft, without your boots or straps.
