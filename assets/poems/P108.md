I wrote a sonnet made of rising smoke,
each line a signal curling toward the blue,
I sent it up before the morning broke,
in hopes that someone far away that grew.

The shapes it made were never quite the letters,
just twisting hints of what I meant to say,
but signals, like the sonnets of our betters,
don't need plain words to find their proper way.

I've watched the smoke dissolve before it's answered,
and wondered if the message ever lands,
but even sonnets sent by fire and cinder
still count as something reaching toward your hands.

So I'll keep sending signals made of embers,
in fourteen curling lines of hopeful gray,
and trust that someone, somewhere, still remembers
to read the smoke the old, unspoken way.
