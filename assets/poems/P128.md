The static age has learned to hum you softly,
a lullaby of signal, noise, and hum,
it rocks the tired minds that scroll too often,
until the quiet, gray oblivion comes.

I've fallen half asleep inside its hissing,
this modern cradle built of screens and wire,
the static age don't notice what we're missing,
it only sings until our eyes grow tired.

They say the lullabies of old were gentle,
sung soft by voices, not by humming light,
but static age has grown so accidental
in how it lulls us into borrowed night.

So let it hum, this static age's cradle,
I'll close my eyes and drift on gray and blue,
some lullabies were never meant for ladle
love, just noise enough to carry you through.
