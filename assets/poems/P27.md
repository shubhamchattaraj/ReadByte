The dunes are made of shattered, shimmering glass,
They sparkle underneath the midday sun.
The shadows of the desert spirits pass,
Until the day of burning heat is done.
The ground is sharp against the weary foot,
Where fire left its deep and blackened soot.

The wind is howling like a wounded beast,
It carves the glass into a thousand shapes.
The hunger of the earth has now increased,
While from the heat the light of day escapes.
The horizon is a mirror in the haze,
Where all the senses vanish in the maze.

I walk across the shards of broken light,
And search for water in the empty wells.
The stars emerge to rule the coming night,
Above the chime of distant, chiming bells.
The silence is a pressure on the ear,
That brings the ghost of all my hidden fear.

The morning dawn will bring the fire back,
To melt the glass beneath a burning sky.
There is no path along this golden track,
Where only dreams of water come to die.
The desert is a kingdom built of pride,
With nowhere left for anyone to hide.
