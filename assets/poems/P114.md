The kerosene sings hymns of amber burning,
a lantern's hum before the wick ignites,
I've smelled that hymn on nights of slow returning,
walking home beneath uncertain lights.

It isn't holy, but it feels like worship,
this quiet fuel that waits to be a flame,
the kerosene hymn hums a kind of courtship
between the dark and light that has no name.

I've filled my lamps with hope as much as fuel,
and struck the match to hear the hymn begin,
some prayers are lit by kerosene, not jewel,
and burn just bright enough to let you in.

So light the wick and let the hymn keep singing,
a small bright hum against the wide dark night,
some faith is only kerosene and bringing
the smallest flame to prove that there is light.
