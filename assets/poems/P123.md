I have a confession pulled beneath the surface,
a secret undertow I can't deny,
it drags at every calm and steady purpose,
and asks me questions I can't clarify.

I've tried to swim against its quiet pulling,
to keep the story simple, calm, and clean,
but confessions have a way of slowly culling
the truth from every surface in between.

They say it's safer keeping things unspoken,
to let the undertow stay hushed and still,
but silence, too, has ways of leaving broken
the shore it swore it never meant to spill.

So let the undertow confess its pulling,
I'd rather drown in truth than float in lies,
some currents aren't worth quietly annulling,
they're only asking that we recognize.
