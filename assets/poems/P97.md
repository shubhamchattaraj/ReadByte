The frost has written anthems on the pane,
in letters only morning learns to read,
a hymn composed of cold and quiet strain,
sung soft by wind that has no other need.

I've stood beneath that anthem, breath like smoke,
and felt the melody move through my chest,
it wasn't warm, but still it gently spoke
of winters that refuse to be suppressed.

The frostbitten anthem doesn't beg for praise,
it only asks that someone stop and hear,
the way the silence bends through icy days,
and turns to music what could be mere fear.

So let it sing, this anthem carved in frost,
I'll listen till the thaw takes it away,
some songs are only meant to sing what's lost,
before they melt into the waking day.
