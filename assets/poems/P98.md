I swore an oath in liquid, shifting metal,
a quicksilver vow that wouldn't hold a shape,
it slipped between my fingers like a petal
of promise poured too fast for me to take.

I chased it round the table of my making,
this vow that wouldn't settle into form,
each time I thought I'd caught it, it was breaking
into a hundred smaller vows, unsworn.

They say a promise ought to be unmoving,
a solid thing you carve into the years,
but mine was born of silver, always proving
too quick to catch, too bright to hold in tears.

So maybe vows were never meant for keeping
in one fixed shape that never learns to bend,
I'll chase my quicksilver, always leaping,
toward a promise with no settled end.
