Upon the shore I built a kingdom small,
from driftwood pale and bleached by salt and sun,
its towers lean, its bridges bound to fall,
but for a while, the kingdom's mine, and won.

I crowned myself with kelp and broken shell,
and ruled the tide pools like they were my land,
the gulls above would sometimes come to tell
me stories of the kingdoms built on sand.

They say a kingdom's only strong in stone,
that driftwood castles never last for long,
but mine was never built to stand alone,
it only had to hold one summer's song.

So let the tide erase my driftwood throne,
I'll build again tomorrow, piece by piece,
a kingdom doesn't need to be full grown,
to give a boy an afternoon of peace.
