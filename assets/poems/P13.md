The ancient pines begin to softly sway,
Beneath the velvet cloak of twilight blue.
The silver moon begins its gentle play,
While shadows dance as day is born anew.
The river hums a melody so deep,
Before the forest settles into sleep.

A rustling branch reveals a hidden flight,
Of owls that watch with eyes of amber fire.
They vanish in the thickness of the night,
As starlight hums upon a crystal lyre.
The mossy floor is cool against the skin,
Where quiet dreams and gentle thoughts begin.

The dawn will break with threads of golden haze,
To wake the ferns that shiver in the dew.
The woodland path becomes a sunlit maze,
Where everything feels tender, fresh, and new.
The spirit of the earth is wide and vast,
A sanctuary built to surely last.
