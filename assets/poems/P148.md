The cartographer left ash instead of ink,
his final map was burned before it's read,
I've tried to trace the lines before they sink
completely into gray and quiet dread.

He mapped the places only fire remembers,
the roads that burned before the town could name,
his ashen charts, in cooling gray Decembers,
still hold the shape of everywhere the flame.

They say a cartographer draws what's living,
the roads still walked, the rivers still in flow,
but his last map was only made from giving
shape to the places embers used to know.

So I'll preserve his cartographer's ashes,
the final map no living hand could keep,
some charts are only drawn from what still flashes
before the fire finally falls asleep.
