The last cartographer of light is walking,
mapping out the edges where it dies,
he doesn't need a voice for careful talking,
just steady hands, and quiet, watchful eyes.

I've followed him through hallways made of shadow,
past corners where the light gives up its hold,
he charts the dark the way one charts a meadow,
in patient lines of silver, gray, and gold.

They say the light will always find a mapper,
someone to trace its edges and its glow,
but even light runs out, and no one's dapper
enough to chart the dark that starts to grow.

So let him walk, this final quiet mapper,
of every place the light has learned to leave,
some cartographers are only meant to capture
the last, soft edge of what we still believe.
