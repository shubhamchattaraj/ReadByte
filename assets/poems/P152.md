The rust has drawn its own uneven heaven,
a constellation spreading cross the beam,
each orange fleck a star however weathered,
connecting in a way I've come to deem.

I've traced the pattern with my finger, tracing
the slow decay as if it told a myth,
the rust constellation, patient, facing
the sky it makes from nothing but the pith.

They say the stars are fixed, eternal, burning,
too far away for rust to ever reach,
but here's a sky of oxidation, turning
the metal into something like a beach.

So let the rust keep drawing constellations,
across the beams that no one thinks to see,
some skies are only made from slow relations
between the water, air, and rusting steel.
