The ashfall drifts like snow that chose to darken,
a quiet serenade of soot and gray,
it falls on rooftops, doesn't stop to hearken
to any voice that begs it stay away.

I've stood beneath it, let it dust my shoulders,
this serenade composed of smoke and heat,
it sings of forests turned to blackened boulders,
a hymn no one had ever meant to meet.

But even ashfall settles into stillness,
and something green will push up through the char,
the serenade, for all its smoky illness,
still hums a tune of what returning are.

So let the ashfall play its somber verses,
I'll wait beneath it for the quiet end,
and trust the song, for all its dark rehearsals,
was always building toward a greener bend.
