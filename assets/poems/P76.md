The wind plays music through the driftwood bones,
a hollow orchestra along the shore,
each rib a flute, each broken plank that moans
a cello note I've never heard before.

I sat among the wreckage, listening close,
to symphonies composed by salt and time,
the bones don't need a conductor's pose,
they play together, wild and out of rhyme.

They say the dead don't sing, but I've heard proof,
the bone orchestra hums beneath the waves,
it plays a song both tender and aloof,
a requiem for shipwrecks turned to graves.

So walk the shore and listen for the sound,
of driftwood strings and wind through hollow chest,
the bone orchestra plays without a bound,
a song for those the ocean laid to rest.
