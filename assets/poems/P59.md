The well is deep and lined with ancient stone,
A fountain of the truth that lies below.
I guard the secrets that I’ve always known,
From all the people that I’ve come to know.
The water ripples with a silver light,
Within the shadow of the velvet night.

I draw the bucket from the cold abyss,
And drink the nectar of the earth’s own heart.
It feels just like a long-forgotten kiss,
That tears the fabric of the world apart.
The keeper knows the truth of all the things,
And hears the music that the water sings.

I sit upon the edge and watch the stars,
Reflected in the mirror of the deep.
It heals the memory of all the scars,
And gives me promise of a dreamless sleep.
The well is older than the human land,
And holds the wisdom in a steady hand.

The morning sun will dry the bucket rim,
And leave the well in silence and in grey.
I watch the way the heavy shadows dim,
Before the ending of the final day.
The keeper is resting by the well,
With all the stories that he has to tell.
