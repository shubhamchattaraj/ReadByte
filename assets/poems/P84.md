I built a church from panes of colored glass,
no walls of stone, just light that filters through,
the congregation is the wind that passed,
and every pew is empty, cold, and blue.

The sun becomes my only preacher here,
it paints the floor in fractured, holy hues,
I sit alone and let the colors clear
my mind of every doubt I didn't choose.

I come here when the weight of living's heavy,
to sit beneath the colors, calm and small,
the glass cathedral doesn't ask a levy,
it only asks that I remember, that's all.

So if you ever find a church of glass,
don't wait for sermons, don't expect a choir,
just let the colored light of morning pass
across your face, and warm you like a fire.
