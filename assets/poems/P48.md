The valley is a cradle in the earth,
A peaceful refuge from the world of noise.
It celebrates the meaning of the birth,
And all the simplicity of human joys.
The river winds a ribbon through the green,
Within the prettiest place I’ve ever seen.

I sit upon the bank and watch the flow,
And feel the rhythm of the living land.
I know the things that I am meant to know,
And hold the wisdom in my steady hand.
The valley is a sanctuary kept,
Where all the spirits of the earth have slept.

The hills are watching over all the rest,
With giants made of granite and of stone.
They give the earth the very, very best,
To leave the world of worry far alone.
The valley is a blessing to the heart,
Before the autumn tears the world apart.

The night is coming with a gentle breeze,
That whispers in the needles of the pine.
It brings the spirit to its tired knees,
To share the beauty of the grand design.
The valley is silent in the midnight air,
With nothing left but what the spirits share.
