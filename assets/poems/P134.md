There's a constellation made of empty spaces,
the stars between the stars that no one draws,
a hollow shape that no one ever traces,
because it's built from nothing, not from laws.

I've found a kind of comfort in its hollow,
a sky that doesn't ask me to compete,
its emptiness is easier to follow,
than patterns built on light and forced conceit.

They say a constellation needs its stars lit,
a shape you connect dot to shining dot,
but mine exists in negative, unfarlit,
a hollow shape of everything I'm not.

So let me trace the hollow constellation,
the dark between the light I'll never own,
some skies are only made for contemplation
of all the empty distance we call home.
