A fish with copper fins is swimming fast,
Through rivers made of liquid mercury.
It leaves behind a shadow of the past,
And sings to clouds above a silent sea.
The trees are growing downward from the sky,
Where birds made out of paper try to fly.

The kitchen table dances in the square,
While cups of tea turn into floating glass.
There is a smell of purple in the air,
As moments like a slow procession pass.
The mirrors are reflecting empty space,
Where someone once had left a missing face.

A dream of velvet doors begins to fold,
Within the pocket of a coat made thin.
The stories are a fabric that is old,
Where endings start to quietly begin.
The logic turns to liquid in the heat,
And leaves us standing on the shifting street.
