The fields are waves of gold beneath the sun,
A bounty that the earth has given wide.
The work of all the summer has been done,
With nothing left but beauty to provide.
The stalks are swaying in the gentle breeze,
Beside the orchard of the apple trees.

We gather in the produce of the land,
A celebration of the food we sow.
We hold the soil within a steady hand,
And watch the way the living spirits grow.
The harvest is a blessing for the heart,
Before the winter tears the world apart.

The silos stand like towers in the light,
A storage for the coming of the cold.
We work until the closing of the night,
And trade the silver for the solid gold.
The table is a bounty for us all,
Within the center of the harvest hall.

The winter wind is waiting for its turn,
To strip the fields and leave the ground as bare.
The lessons that we work so hard to learn,
Are hanging in the cooling autumn air.
The harvest ends its cycle in the light,
And leaves us ready for the coming night.
