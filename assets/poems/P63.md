The wolf moon climbs above the frozen pine,
its silver breath falls soft on sleeping snow,
somewhere a howl runs down the tree line,
calling to a world it used to know.

I've heard that cry from rooms too far from wild,
a hunger caged inside a borrowed skin,
the moon don't care if you're a grown or child,
it pulls the tide of blood that waits within.

I stood outside and let the cold come in,
and felt my pulse fall into that old song,
the moon don't judge the color of your skin,
it only asks how long you've waited, how long.

So when it rises, full and white and wide,
I'll answer back the only way I can,
with something wordless, something I can't hide,
the wolf that lives beneath the quiet man.
