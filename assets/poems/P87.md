There's a constellation no one's ever named,
a scatter of wild stars that won't be tamed,
they shift each night, refuse to hold a shape,
a feral sky that always will escape.

I've tried to draw the lines that connect them,
a bear, a hunter, something I invent,
but every night the pattern's different,
as if the stars themselves are not content.

I lie beneath it, watch the wild stars turning,
no order, no constraint, no fixed design,
and feel a kind of freedom, slow and burning,
in watching something refuse to fall in line.

So let the feral constellation roam,
don't try to cage it in a human name,
some skies were never meant to find a home,
in patterns we invent to play our game.
