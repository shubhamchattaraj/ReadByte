The skyline bleeds with static, harsh and bright,
A grid of wires beneath the heavy rain.
The city pulse is hidden from the light,
Inside the mainframe of a digital brain.
We trade our souls for bandwidth and for speed,
While hungry algorithms start to feed.

The holograms are flickering in grey,
Reflecting off the steel and rusted glass.
We watch the ghost machines begin to play,
As data streams in torrents quickly pass.
The firewall is closing round the door,
But we don't remember what we're searching for.

A circuit board is beating in my chest,
Synthetic heartbeat echoing the code.
I never find the time for quiet rest,
Upon this paved and cold electric road.
The future is a glitch within the screen,
A memory of things that might have been.
