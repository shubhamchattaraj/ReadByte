The frost writes numbers on the window glass,
a ledger kept in crystal, cold and neat,
it counts the days that slowly learn to pass,
and tallies up the hours of the sleet.

I read the frost the way you'd read a debt,
each line a record of the falling snow,
the winter doesn't ever seem to fret,
it simply writes what it already knows.

I've paid my share in shivers, breath, and frost,
the ledger doesn't care how much it costs,
but something in the waiting isn't lost,
it's only saved for when the warmth is tossed.

So let the winter keep its careful book,
I'll wait until the numbers start to thaw,
and watch the spring arrive to overlook
the frozen debt that winter's ledger saw.
