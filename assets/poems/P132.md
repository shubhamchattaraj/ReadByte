I am the last to chart these fading borders,
the final hand to draw a river's bend,
the world has hired satellites for orders,
and paper maps have found their quiet end.

But still I sketch the coastlines with my fingers,
and shade the mountains soft in graphite gray,
some part of mapping's magic always lingers,
in hands that draw instead of hands that play.

They say the world's been measured, charted, finished,
that nothing's left unmarked upon the earth,
but I still find the corners undiminished,
where cartographers can prove their private worth.

So let me be the last one drawing borders,
by candlelight, with ink instead of code,
the final cartographer of orders,
still drawing maps for one more, unmapped road.
