The sun today is thin, threadbare, and worn,
its light comes through like fabric stretched too far,
I've watched it rise, uncertain and forlorn,
a paler version of the sun we are.

It doesn't burn the way it used to burn,
it filters soft through clouds that dim its gold,
I wonder if the sun itself will learn
to fade completely, threadbare, tired, and old.

Perhaps the sun, like all of us, grows weary,
perhaps its light was never meant to last
at full and blazing strength, forever cheery,
but softens, threadbare, as the years go past.

So let the threadbare sun still rise this morning,
I'll take its paler light without complaint,
there's beauty in a light that gives no warning,
that even suns grow tired, grow worn, grow faint.
