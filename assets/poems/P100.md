I've played the understudy to the falling,
the one who waits backstage while gravity
performs the role that no one else is calling,
the pull that always gets the final say.

I've watched it take the lead in every story,
the apple, and the tide, and falling rain,
while I rehearse a line about the glory
of things that rise, and never quite explain.

Someday they'll need an understudy's talent,
for holding steady when the pull gives way,
I'll step up quiet, patient, and gallant,
and show the stage that lightness has its day.

Until that curtain calls my name for rising,
I'll study gravity's practiced, patient fall,
and learn the weight behind its unsurprising,
insistent, steady answer to it all.
