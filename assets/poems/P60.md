The wind is spinning thread of invisible, 
A loom of air that stretches to the sky.
I catch the pieces of the sensible,
And watch the moments as they drift and fly.
The weaver is a ghost within the breeze,
That whispers through the tops of ancient trees.

I pull the threads of thunder and of rain,
And weave them into patterns of the storm.
It carries all the pleasure and the pain,
To keep the fragile heart within me warm.
The wind is singing in a steady tone,
A song that I have claimed to be my own.

The tapestry is hanging in the air,
A work of art that no one else can see.
It speaks of all the things that we can share,
Of all the things that we are meant to be.
The weaver is a dancer in the light,
That spins the fabric of the coming night.

The morning light will tear the cloth away,
And leave the sky in silence and in blue.
I wait until the ending of the day,
To start the work that I am meant to do.
The weaver of the wind is gone at last,
And left the fabric for the heavy past.
