The iron sings a lullaby of rusting,
a ferrous hum too low for waking ears,
it cradles bolts and beams that keep adjusting
to gravity, and all its slow, patient years.

I've heard it in the bones of bridges, sleeping,
a hymn composed of oxide, rain, and rest,
the ferrous lullaby keeps quiet keeping,
the girders calm inside their metal chest.

They say that rust is only slow decaying,
a certain end that no repair can stop,
but in its hum there's something soft and staying,
a lullaby that doesn't ever drop.

So let the iron hum its rusting verses,
I'll rest beneath the bridges, calm and still,
and trust the lullaby, for all its curses,
is only rust learning to be still.
