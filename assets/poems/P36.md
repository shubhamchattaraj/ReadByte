The frost is creeping on the window pane,
A lace of crystal in the winter light.
It hides the ghost of all the spring-time rain,
Beneath the heavy blanket of the white.
The world is silent in the frozen air,
With nothing left but what the spirits share.

The trees are skeletons against the grey,
Their branches reaching for the empty sky.
We wait until the ending of the day,
To watch the spirit of the summer die.
The ice is thick upon the river bed,
Where words of winter are all finally said.

The sun is low and pale above the hill,
A token of a light that’s fading fast.
The world is turning slow and standing still,
To make the final breath of winter last.
The snow is falling softly on the ground,
Without a single whisper or a sound.

The winter is the end of all the strife,
A period of rest before the spring.
It takes away the busy noise of life,
And leaves us with the song that we can sing.
The breath is final in the cooling air,
Before the world begins to grow so fair.
