I sail upon a sea of words,
With paper sails and quill for mast,
To chase the flight of silent birds,
Across a kingdom fading fast.
The ink is deep, the tide is wide,
A journey carved in rhythmic prose,
With nowhere left for me to hide,
Within the petals of a rose.

The ocean swells with hidden themes,
Of battles fought in ink and stain,
Of sailors lost in waking dreams,
And mountains built of blue and rain.
The compass needle spins in doubt,
As adjectives begin to blur,
And every sentence wanders out,
To catch a whisper, then a stir.

I drift past islands made of breath,
Where metaphors grow thick and tall,
Defying silence, fearing death,
Before the final ink-drop fall.
The syntax holds the hull in place,
A rhythmic beat against the keel,
While moving through the empty space,
To find out what is truly real.

And when the harbor comes in view,
With docks of parchment, soft and white,
I close the book and start anew,
To lose myself within the light.
The voyage ends where it began,
Upon the page, within the pen,
The map drawn by a mortal man,
Who lives to sail it all again.
