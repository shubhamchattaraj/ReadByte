The mirror shows a face I do not know,
A fracture in the glass of what I see.
The memories of all the long ago,
Are trapped within the ghost of what was me.
The light is bouncing off the silver back,
Along the line of every jagged crack.

I see the world in pieces on the wall,
A shattered vision of the way it seems.
I hear the echo of a distant call,
That haunts the stillness of my waking dreams.
The reflection is a stranger in the room,
Within the shadow of the coming gloom.

I try to pick the pieces from the floor,
To make the image whole and clear again.
But there is nothing I am looking for,
Except the memory of all the pain.
The shards are sharp against the finger tip,
And make the world of logic slowly slip.

The glass is dusty in the dimming light,
And shows me nothing but the empty space.
I walk away into the coming night,
Without the comfort of a known face.
The reflections remain broken on the wall,
To signify the ending of it all.
