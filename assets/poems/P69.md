The harbor smells of salt and burning wood,
a fire on the pier where sailors gather,
they tell their tales the way old sailors should,
of storms they've weathered, skies gone dark and rather.

I sat among them, listening to the waves,
crash soft against the hull of tired boats,
the smoke curled up like ghosts from salted graves,
and mixed its gray with mist along their coats.

The sea don't care for stories that we tell,
it takes the smoke and salt without a sound,
but still we sit and ring the fire's bell,
and pass the night till morning comes around.

So when you smell the salt mixed in with smoke,
you'll know a fire's burning by the shore,
where sailors sit and share the words they spoke,
about the sea, and what it's taken, and what's still in store.
