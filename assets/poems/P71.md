A feather forged from iron, heavy, cold,
fell from a bird that no one saw take flight,
it landed hard, its story yet untold,
a weight too dark to lift into the light.

I picked it up and felt the ancient rust,
a relic of some sky that turned to stone,
it whispered of the wind it learned to trust,
before the iron claimed it for its own.

I kept the feather, heavy in my hand,
a piece of flight that gravity still owns,
I couldn't fly, but still I understand
the weight of wings, the iron in their bones.

So if you find a feather forged in iron,
don't toss it back to earth and walk away,
hold on to what the fallen sky requires,
and carry iron wings into your day.
