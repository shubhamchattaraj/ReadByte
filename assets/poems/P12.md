He sits upon a throne of fused-glass veins,
Where molten stars are shackled into chains.
His crown—a ring of pulsars, cold and bright—
Commands the surging currents of the night.
Below, the constellations bend the knee,
To one who holds the master-memory.

"I built the silence!" thunders forth his voice,
A sound that left the cosmos little choice.
He tore the gravity from dying suns,
To feed the hunger of his burning ones.
No throne of flesh, no crown of brittle gold,
Could house the fury that he dared to hold.

The galaxy is bowing at his feet,
A symphony of terror, stark and sweet.
He wove the space-time fabric into cord,
The undisputed, unmade, iron lord.
And as he drinks the embers of the spark,
He laughs—and swallows whole the screaming dark.
