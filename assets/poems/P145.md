The stars are slowly coming undone tonight,
their old formations drifting out of place,
the myths we hung on them don't hold up right,
against the way the sky moves through its space.

I've watched the hunter's belt begin to scatter,
the bear's old shape no longer quite the same,
the undoing of stars is all that matters
when nothing in the sky stays fixed, or tame.

They say the constellations tell our stories,
a fixed reflection of the myths we keep,
but even sky forgets its inventories,
and drifts away while all the old gods sleep.

So let the stars keep coming slowly undone,
I'll learn new shapes as old ones drift and fade,
some skies were never meant to stay as one
fixed picture that our stories always made.
