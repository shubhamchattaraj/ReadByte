I built a cathedral out of hiss and hollow,
between the stations where no voices reach,
its aisles are static, and the pews I follow
are made of silence dressed in gray and bleach.

No choir sings here, only frequencies,
a hum too soft to call a hymn or prayer,
but still I kneel among the silences,
and feel something like worship in the air.

They say this church has no god to believe in,
just interference, empty, cold, and vast,
but I have found a kind of peace in grieving
inside a place that holds no future, past.

So let the static cathedral keep its hollow,
I'll visit when the noisy world gets loud,
and in its quiet gray, I'll always follow
the emptiness that never draws a crowd.
