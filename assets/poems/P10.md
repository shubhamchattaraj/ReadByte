The pistons are pounding a beat in the deep,
Where shadows awaken while guardians sleep.
With metal that rattles and gears that all grind,
We’re leaving the sunlight and surface behind.
The rhythm is heavy, the rhythm is cold,
A story of brass that is yet to be told.

Click-clack goes the lever, hiss-wheeze goes the steam,
We’re chasing the ghost of a dying star's dream.
The mercury pulses in veins made of lead,
While binary whispers dance round in my head.
The drum of the hull in the belly of night,
Is keeping the time for our fast, lonely flight.

With voltage that surges and wires that hum,
We march to the beat of a synthetic drum.
The stars are but sparks in a vacuum of glass,
As seconds and centuries quietly pass.
So steady the motion and rhythmic the flow,
Where the iron hearts beat and the core-fires glow.

The cycle is endless, the pattern is set,
A dance of the atoms we shouldn't forget.
Through voids of the silence where gravity wanes,
The pulse of the engine still thrums in our veins.
It’s steady, it’s rhythmic, it’s constant, it’s true—
The song of the ship as it sails into blue.
