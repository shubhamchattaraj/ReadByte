The sea turns amber underneath the dusk,
a golden pull beneath the surface calm,
it drags the light down soft, like burning husk
of sunset, drowning slow without alarm.

I've felt that undertow along my ribs,
a warmth that pulls me toward the fading gold,
it isn't cruel, it only slowly ribs
the day away, the way the tide is told.

I stood knee-deep and let it wrap my legs,
this honeyed water pulling at the shore,
it asked for nothing, only what it begs
the sun to give it, nothing less, no more.

So when the evening turns the water gold,
don't fight the pull of amber underneath,
just let it take you, gentle, calm and old,
into the color of the day's last breath.
