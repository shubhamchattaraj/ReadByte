We met beneath a moon that's only halfway,
a contraband of light the night let slip,
we didn't ask permission for our pathway,
just walked it soft, and let the silence dip.

The other half was hidden, dark, unspoken,
like every secret that we didn't share,
but half a moon was proof enough, unbroken,
that light can smuggle softness through the air.

I've kept that halfmoon folded in my pocket,
a contraband of memory and glow,
no customs house could ever come to block it,
it's mine to carry everywhere I go.

So let the halfmoon stay our quiet secret,
a smuggled light too soft for anyone,
some nights don't need the whole moon to complete it,
just half a light, and knowing where you've gone.
