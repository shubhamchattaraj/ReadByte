The floorboards groan beneath a phantom tread,
Though no one walks within the hollow hall.
I shiver in the safety of my bed,
And watch the shadows stretch against the wall.
The air is thick with scent of damp and rot,
As if the world has long since been forgot.

A sudden chill descends upon my skin,
I hear a whisper close against my ear.
The nightmare has already started in,
And stripped away my capacity for fear.
The locks are turning one by one tonight,
As something hungry hides beyond the light.

I hold my breath and stare into the dark,
Where eyes of void are watching every move.
I cannot find a single saving spark,
Or anything that I might yet approve.
The door swings open with a lonely creak,
And now I find the words I cannot speak.
