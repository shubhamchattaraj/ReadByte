The blueprints are a work of grand design,
A vision for the towers in the sky.
I draw the curves of every flowing line,
And watch the clouds as they begin to fly.
The structure rises from the empty space,
With beauty and with elegance and grace.

I build the rooms of memory and gold,
To house the spirits of the long ago.
The stories of the future that were told,
Are places that the mortals ought to know.
The architect is working with the mind,
To leave the world of ignorance behind.

The halls are filled with echoes of the past,
And corridors that wind through time and light.
The building is a monument to last,
Within the belly of the endless night.
The dreams are solid as the granite wall,
Before the inevitable rise and fall.

The morning light will shatter all the view,
And leave the world in silence and in grey.
I start the work of building all anew,
Before the ending of the final day.
The architect is resting in the dream,
Beside the quiet of the flowing stream.
