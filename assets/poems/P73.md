A single spark against a page of white,
and suddenly the words begin to burn,
the story catches, races through the night,
each sentence turning faster than I learn.

I tried to fold the fire, keep it small,
but paper wildfires don't obey the hand,
they climb the margins, leap the wall to wall,
and turn my quiet room to burning land.

They say a fire this bright can't last for long,
that paper burns and paper turns to dust,
but something in the ash still holds a song,
a whisper of the flame I couldn't trust.

So let it burn, this wildfire made of pages,
I'll write again when morning clears the smoke,
the story lives through all its burning stages,
and rises new from every word it spoke.
