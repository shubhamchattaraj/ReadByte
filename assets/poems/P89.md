The edge of evening wears a coat of sable,
a black horizon stitched from fading light,
I watch it rise as if it's somehow able
to swallow day and hand it over to night.

The birds fly low beneath that sable seam,
their shadows blending into what remains,
the world grows quiet, caught between a dream
and waking, held by soft and sable chains.

Perhaps it's not a line that's meant for finding,
perhaps it only asks that I take part,
in walking toward a darkness, softly binding
the fading light around my quiet heart.

So let the sable horizon keep retreating,
I'll chase it slow, without the need to win,
some journeys aren't designed for ever meeting,
they're only meant to draw the dusk within.
