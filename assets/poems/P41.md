The ink is flowing from the silver pen,
A river made of thought and heavy lead.
It writes the stories of the world of men,
Before the words are left to be unsaid.
The paper is a vessel for the soul,
That makes the broken parts of spirit whole.

I track the movement of the hand in flight,
Across the surface of the parchment white.
It captures all the visions of the night,
And turns the darkness into blinding light.
The pen is guided by a steady ghost,
Who whispers to the writer and the host.

The sentences are winding in a chain,
A logical and beautiful design.
They speak of all the pleasure and the pain,
And draw a clear and everlasting line.
The ink is dry upon the page at last,
To join the collection of the heavy past.

The story is a legacy to keep,
Within the library of human mind.
It wakes the world from all its heavy sleep,
And leaves the ignorance so far behind.
The pen is resting now upon the desk,
To leave a mark that’s quite picturesque.
