There's geometry in every drift of ashes,
angles measured out by wind and chance,
each falling flake obeys the math it clashes
with gravity in its uncertain dance.

I've studied every curve and every scatter,
the way the gray falls softer than it seems,
the geometry of ash is all that matters
when measuring the shape of burning dreams.

They say geometry is fixed and certain,
a truth of angles, planes, and perfect lines,
but ash draws different shapes behind the curtain,
uncertain proofs and soft, uneven signs.

So let me study every falling angle,
the geometry that fire leaves behind,
some math is only meant to help untangle
the shape of loss, and how it's redefined.
