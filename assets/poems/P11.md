The marrow screams in a house of chrome,
Where nerves are wires and the skin is loam.
It’s not a malfunction; it’s a violent birth—
The tearing of sky from the crust of the earth.
The pressure is peaking!
The structure is shrieking!
The sanity-sealant is starting to crack,
And there is no throttle to pull us back.

Total collapse in a micro-second,
To the edge of the pit where the void has beckoned.
My pulse is a strobe in the strobe-lit dark,
A frantic, dying, electrical spark.
It’s cold! It’s burning! It’s violently bright!
The physics are failing to hold back the night.
The atoms are screaming as they tear at the seam,
Waking the god from a feverish dream.

Silence, now.
Absolute, ringing, absolute zero.
Not a breath.
Not a thought.
Just the crushing, expanding, terminal weight
Of a universe closing its heavy, iron gate.
You are the marrow.
You are the bone.
And you are being shattered...
all...
on...
your...
own.
