The winter packed its bags before the springtime,
a runaway who couldn't stand to stay,
it left the frost behind without a longtime
goodbye, just slipping quietly away.

I've watched it go each year with mixed emotion,
relief, and something close to soft regret,
the runaway winter, calm devotion
to leaving just as soon as it is met.

They say that seasons don't have choice in leaving,
they're bound by tilt and orbit, cold and light,
but I imagine winter's own believing
that leaving early makes the exit right.

So let the winter run before its ending,
I'll wave it off with equal parts of grace,
some seasons benefit from early sending,
before they overstay their frozen place.
