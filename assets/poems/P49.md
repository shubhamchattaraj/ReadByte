The furnace glows with heat of liquid fire,
A dance of sand transformed to fragile art.
I build the shapes of every deep desire,
And hold the molten beauty in my heart.
The cooling air begins to set the form,
To keep the inner spirit safe and warm.

I pull the rod to stretch the burning light,
And shape the glass with steady, practiced hand.
It glimmers in the shadows of the night,
Like treasures buried in the desert sand.
The vessel waits to capture all the breath,
To keep the memory from the touch of death.

I watch the color bloom within the frame,
A swirl of cobalt and a streak of gold.
It flickers like the ghost of dying flame,
And tells the secrets that were never told.
The glass is cooling on the wooden rack,
With no desire for looking safely back.

The finished piece is clear against the sky,
A work of patience and of quiet skill.
I watch the swallows as they start to fly,
Above the cresting of the distant hill.
The weaver rests until the dawn is new,
To start the work that I am meant to do.
