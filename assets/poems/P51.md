The compass needle points to distant stars,
A guide for all the wanderers of earth.
It carries all the memories of scars,
And celebrates the meaning of the birth.
The path is winding through the valley deep,
Where secrets of the ancient spirits sleep.

I walk the road that leads to nowhere fast,
And watch the shadows lengthen on the plain.
The ghosts of all the things that surely passed,
Are whispering within the summer rain.
The compass is a steady, faithful friend,
Upon which all my lonely steps depend.

The map is worn and tattered at the edge,
From all the years of traveling the land.
I stand upon the narrow granite ledge,
And hold the future in a steady hand.
The world is vast and open to the view,
Where everything is feeling fresh and new.

The journey ends beneath the setting sun,
In places that I never thought to see.
The race of life is finally all done,
And I am left with who I want to be.
The compass is now resting in the chest,
To give the weary traveler some rest.
