A requiem in amber, slow and golden,
plays soft for everything the light lets go,
the day's last hour, warm and softly olden,
sings out before it fades to indigo.

I've sat beneath that requiem, unmoving,
and watched the amber turn to something dim,
it doesn't rush, it's patient in its proving
that endings, too, deserve a gentle hymn.

They say a requiem is meant for mourning,
a heavy song for heavy, grieving hearts,
but amberlight requiems, soft and warning,
are gentler than the grief that mourning starts.

So let the amberlight keep singing softly,
I'll watch the day dissolve to violet skies,
some requiems are only sung to gently
remind us beauty's found in slow goodbyes.
