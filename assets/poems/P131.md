There's a station broadcasting from the thicket,
a wolfsbane frequency, low and unclear,
it plays a warning if you learn to pick it
out from the static hiding in your ear.

I've tuned in nights when danger felt uncertain,
and heard the wolfsbane whisper through the wire,
it doesn't shout, it only draws the curtain
back on the shapes that prowl beyond the fire.

They say the plant was named for wolves it poisoned,
a purple bloom that carries its own dread,
the radio that bears its name, unchosen,
still hums the warnings older ones have said.

So keep the dial tuned to wolfsbane's station,
you'll hear it if the danger's drawing near,
some frequencies exist for preservation,
of those who learn to listen, wide and clear.
