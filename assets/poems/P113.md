The frost on glass becomes a kind of magic,
a window turned to alchemy by cold,
it draws in ferns and patterns, sad or tragic,
in silver lines no hand could ever hold.

I've pressed my palm against that frozen wonder,
and felt the heat begin to melt its spell,
the alchemy of frost breaks slowly under
the warmth of skin, and that's a story too, to tell.

They say alchemists could turn to gold,
what once was simple, common, base, and plain,
but windowpane alchemy needs nothing bold,
just cold, and time, and glass to hold the strain.

So watch the window when the frost is working,
its quiet alchemy of ice and light,
some magic doesn't need a wizard lurking,
it only needs the cold and still of night.
