The stars are stretching thin like pulled-out wire,
We drift beyond the reach of human light.
Our engines burn with cold and silent fire,
To pierce the belly of the endless night.
The gravity is bending space and time,
A steep and vertical and lonely climb.

We watch the galaxies explode like dust,
A trillion years condensed into a spark.
We leave behind the iron and the rust,
And sail into the ocean of the dark.
The maps are useless in this uncharted sea,
Where physics bends to wild decree.

The ship is humming songs of distant suns,
Of planets born from clouds of spinning gas.
The countdown to the unknown slowly runs,
As through the singularity we pass.
We are the travelers who seek the end,
Where all the cosmic broken lines will mend.
