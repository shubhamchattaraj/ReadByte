The streetlamp flickers, dying in the fog,
The pavement slick with oil and city grime.
I’m just another weary, broken cog,
Who’s running out of patience and of time.
The dame was trouble dressed in velvet lace,
With secrets hidden in her hollow face.

The smoke is curling round my tired eyes,
A bitter taste of whiskey and regret.
The city is a mask of clever lies,
That makes you wish for things you should forget.
The sirens wail a mournful, distant tune,
Beneath the pale and judgmental moon.

I pull my collar tight against the chill,
And walk the alleyways of lost despair.
The world is turning, cold and standing still,
With nothing left but static in the air.
The case is closed but justice isn't found,
Just silence pressing heavily around.
