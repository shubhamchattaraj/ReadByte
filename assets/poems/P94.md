I sing a hymn for everything that's burning,
a wildfire psalm for forests turned to flame,
the ash rises like prayers, slowly turning
to smoke that carries up without a name.

The animals run fast before the burning,
the birds take flight above the reddened hill,
I watch the fire with a strange discerning,
a terrible, undeniable skill.

I don't sing praise for pain, for loss, for burning,
I sing instead for what will rise again,
the wildfire psalm is not for mourning,
it's for the green that follows in its wake.

So let the fire come, let the psalm be spoken,
I'll mourn the loss but trust the seeds beneath,
the wildfire psalm, though terrible and broken,
still promises a forest's second breath.
