The mountain stands against the endless sky,
A titan carved from rock and ancient snow.
We watch the eagles as they start to fly,
Above the valleys where the rivers flow.
The air is thin and crisp upon the height,
Where mountain spirits rule the coming night.

I climb the path that winds along the edge,
And feel the danger of the rocky trail.
I stand upon the narrow granite ledge,
And watch the clouds as they begin to sail.
The world is small from such a distance high,
Beneath the vast and unrelenting sky.

The summit is a challenge to the soul,
That makes the human spirit truly rise.
It helps to make the broken pieces whole,
And shows the wisdom of the ancient wise.
The mountain is a master of the land,
With secrets written in the shifting sand.

The descent is a slow and steady walk,
Back down into the bustle of the town.
I hear the people start to loudly talk,
As evening pulls the golden curtain down.
The mountain is a memory of grace,
That stays within the spirit and the space.
