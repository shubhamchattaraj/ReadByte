The weathervane admits it's never certain,
it spins to please whatever wind blows through,
it wears no shame behind its rusted curtain,
just turns and turns to find a truth that's true.

I've watched it twist from north to south by evening,
uncertain as the gusts that pull it round,
and thought that maybe I've been mildly grieving
a kind of constancy I've never found.

But there's a kind of honesty in turning,
in letting every wind decide your face,
the weathervane's confession, plainly learning
that constancy was never its true place.

So let me spin like weathervanes in autumn,
admitting every gust that comes my way,
there's nothing weak in bending toward the bottom
of whichever wind arrives to have its say.
