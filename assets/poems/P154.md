I rule a kingdom made of ancient vellum,
its borders drawn in ink too faint to read,
the vellum kingdom has no need to swell 'em,
its subjects are the letters, and I lead.

I've walked its halls of parchment, thin and golden,
each page a province I have come to know,
the vellum kingdom, fragile but not olden
beyond repair, keeps every word aglow.

They say a kingdom needs a throne of granite,
a crown of gold to prove its rightful claim,
but mine is only paper, and I plan it
with nothing but a quill to sign my name.

So let the vellum kingdom keep its pages,
I'll rule it gently, careful with each turn,
some kingdoms only last across the ages
if kept as soft as vellum lets them learn.
