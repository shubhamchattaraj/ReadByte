There is a physics to the way I miss you,
a force that pulls no matter the distance grown,
I've done the math a hundred ways to twist you
back into the room where I sit alone.

The equations never balance out completely,
there's always some remainder, some small ache,
the physics of this longing works discreetly,
in variables no formula could break.

They say that distance follows simple reason,
a straight line measured out in miles or years,
but longing doesn't follow any season,
it just keeps solving for undone tears.

So let the physics stay a bit unsolved,
I'll carry the equation everywhere,
some longing was never meant to be resolved,
just carried, like a constant, in the air.
