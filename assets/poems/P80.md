The evening bruises into shades of violet,
a static purple humming through the air,
the day gives up its colors, doesn't fight it,
and lets the dusk take over, unaware.

I've watched the violet climb the fading blue,
like bruises spreading soft across the sky,
it isn't sad, it's only something true,
the color of the day's slow lullaby.

I stood outside and let the color bruise
my skin with something gentle, cool, and deep,
the violet static wasn't mine to lose,
it only came to walk me into sleep.

So watch the sky turn violet, watch it hum,
don't rush the dusk, don't wish the day to stay,
just let the static violet slowly come,
and carry you the quiet, purple way.
