My heart's a chamber built for echoes only,
each word I say comes bouncing back distorted,
the love I send returns a little lonely,
a fainter version of what I imported.

I've learned to speak in softer, careful tones,
so what comes back won't shatter what I meant,
but even whispers turn to hollow moans
inside this chamber where my love is spent.

But even echoes prove that something's there,
a voice that traveled, bounced, and still returned,
this chamber heart, for all its empty air,
still holds the shape of everything I've learned.

So if you hear an echo when I speak,
don't mistake the distance for the lack of true,
it's just the chamber, hollow, worn and weak,
still doing all it can to carry you.
