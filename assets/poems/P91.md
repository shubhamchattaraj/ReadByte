The sky tonight is thin as ancient vellum,
stretched tight across the dark, and faintly torn,
I read the stars like words some careful scholar
inscribed before the modern world was born.

Each constellation is a sentence written,
in light too old for me to understand,
the vellum sky, by centuries so smitten,
still holds the ink of some forgotten hand.

They say the sky's a page that's always turning,
new stories written nightly, overlaid,
but some nights, when the ancient light is burning,
I swear the vellum's older than it's made.

So let me read the sky like ancient vellum,
each star a word too distant to define,
I'll never fully understand the scholar
who wrote the constellations, line by line.
