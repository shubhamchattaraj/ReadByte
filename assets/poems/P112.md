I mapped the spreading orange of the metal,
each blossom marking where the water's been,
a cartography drawn out in petal
patterns only time and rain have seen.

The rust don't follow roads or straight directions,
it blooms wherever moisture finds a seam,
I've traced its slow and patient imperfections,
across the surface of a rusted beam.

They say a map should lead you somewhere certain,
should have a scale, a legend, and a key,
but rust maps only draw the falling curtain,
of everything that used to hold up, free.

So I'll keep charting orange constellations,
across the metal, patient, slow, and true,
the cartography of rust needs no translations,
it only asks that we still find it beautiful.
