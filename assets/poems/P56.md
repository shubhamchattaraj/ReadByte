The gears are ticking in a wooden box,
A gift of love for all the passing time.
I turn the handle on the brassy locks,
And hear the ringing of the iron chime.
The clockmaker is old and bent with years,
But masks his sorrow and his hidden fears.

I watch the hands that move with steady pace,
Across the surface of the ivory face.
I see the kindness in his tired face,
And feel the warmth of his long-lost embrace.
The time is ticking in the hollow shell,
With all the things that he has tried to tell.

I learn to mend the springs and polish steel,
To keep the rhythm of the world in check.
I understand the way the spirits feel,
Before they suffer in the final wreck.
The daughter is the keeper of the craft,
And laughs whenever life has truly laughed.

The clock will strike the hour of the noon,
And fill the room with music of the heart.
The day will pass into the rising moon,
Before we have to tear the world apart.
The clockmaker is sleeping with the gears,
And leaving her to face the coming years.
