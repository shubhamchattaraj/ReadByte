My needle points to nowhere known,
A magnetic pull toward the ghost,
Of seeds that I have never sown,
Along a wild and jagged coast.
It shivers in my trembling hand,
This silver tongue of North and South,
To lead me to a hidden land,
That trembles on the ocean’s mouth.

The sea is ink, the sky is lead,
The stars have hidden from the gale,
The path ahead is marked by dread,
With tattered, thin, and weary sail.
I follow where the spirit leads,
Through heavy mist and driving spray,
To plant the hungry, lonely seeds,
That wait for light of brighter day.

The compass lies, the needle spins,
It seeks a pole that does not exist,
Where every end of time begins,
Within the belly of the mist.
It leads me circles, wide and deep,
Across the spine of sleeping whales,
While all the ocean secrets keep,
Within the tattered, shredded sails.

And if I lose the path to home,
And if the needle breaks in two,
I’ll drift upon the white-capped foam,
With nothing left for me to do.
The ocean claims the final part,
The weary man, the broken map,
And hides the beating of my heart,
Within the ocean’s closing trap.
