The sea turns black beneath the moonless sky,
an obsidian tide that swallows every light,
I stand upon the shore and wonder why
the water chose to wear the color of the night.

It moves like glass, sharp-edged and cold and deep,
reflecting nothing but the dark it holds,
the waves don't crash, they only creep and creep,
across the sand in slow, deliberate folds.

But still I stood and let the black waves reach
my feet, and felt no malice in their pull,
just quiet, ancient patience on the beach,
a tide that's neither cruel nor merciful.

So if you find the sea has turned to glass,
don't fear the dark, don't run from what it holds,
obsidian tides are only night that's passed
into the water, ancient, calm, and old.
