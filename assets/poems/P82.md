The ashes rise and fall in slow rotation,
a waltz of gray across the burning floor,
they spin without a partner, no formation,
just grace in every step forevermore.

I've watched them dance above the dying embers,
three-quarter time in silence, soft and slow,
the fire keeps the rhythm it remembers,
and lets the ashes lead the way they go.

I've joined them once, or maybe just imagined,
a waltz of smoke that pulled me from my chair,
the room went gray, and everything happened
in rhythm with the ashes in the air.

So when you watch a fire slowly dying,
don't look away before the dance is done,
the ashen waltz is worth the time of trying,
to see the beauty in the fading one.
