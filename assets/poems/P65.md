The night comes wrapped in velvet full of hiss,
a hush that hums beneath the quiet air,
I lay awake to catch what I might miss,
the static breathing softly everywhere.

It sounds like rain that never learned to fall,
like whispers caught between two closing doors,
I let it fill the corners of the wall,
and settle soft along the wooden floors.

I've tried to name the color of that sound,
not black, not silver, something in between,
a velvet hush that wraps the world around,
and hides the edges of the things unseen.

So let it hiss, let it hold on to me,
this static velvet stitched from night and air,
it asks for nothing, costs me nothing free,
just steady breathing in the dark somewhere.
