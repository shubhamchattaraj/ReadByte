The glasswing settles soft on broken branches,
its elegy is written in the light
that passes through its body's clear expanses,
a mourning song performed in flight, in flight.

I've watched it land where other wings would shatter,
transparent grief on wood that's turned to gray,
it doesn't seem to mind if wings don't matter,
so long as they can carry it away.

They say the glasswing lives a short existence,
too brief to justify its fragile art,
but every elegy, despite the distance,
still says enough about a fleeting heart.

So let the glasswing sing its clear lamenting,
its body proof that grief can still be light,
some sorrow's meant for briefly, not relenting,
just one soft pass before it turns to flight.
