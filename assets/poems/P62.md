The line where sky and water learn to bend,
is drawn in glass no hand has ever made,
I've watched it break and watched it start again,
each dusk a debt that morning always paid.

The ships pass through like ghosts of appointments kept,
their sails all cut from clouds too thin to hold,
the water underneath has never slept,
it turns the light to liquid, endless gold.

They say horizons run from those who chase,
that glass is glass no matter how it gleams,
but I have seen the way it holds a face,
reflecting back the shape of all my dreams.

So let it bend, let it refuse to break,
I'll walk the shore until the tide is done,
for every edge the quiet dawn will make,
belongs a while to me and to the sun.
