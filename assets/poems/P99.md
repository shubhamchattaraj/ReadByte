There's a station playing songs from paper moons,
a static hush of silver, borrowed light,
it hums the words to old, forgotten tunes,
and broadcasts them across the sleeping night.

I've tuned my dial to catch that fragile sound,
half music and half rustle of the page,
the paper moon keeps turning slow around,
a radio tower built from another age.

The songs don't ask for anything but listening,
they don't demand a name or your reply,
just quiet ears beneath a sky that's glistening
with static stars the paper moon put by.

So if you find that frequency tonight,
don't touch the dial, don't turn it toward the news,
just let the paper moon keep singing light
into the dark, in fragile, silver hues.
