The horses turn upon the gilded pole,
A wooden flight beneath the circus tent.
They play a music for the weary soul,
A song of all the days we have misspent.
The lights are blinking in a rhythmic beat,
Above the empty and the dusty seat.

The ride is spinning faster than the light,
A blur of color in the velvet dark.
We laugh into the belly of the night,
And leave behind a tiny, glowing spark.
The carousel is dancing on the wind,
For all the ones who have so long since sinned.

The faces are a mask of painted joy,
Designed to hide the sorrow in the heart.
We play the role of simple girl and boy,
Before we have to tear the world apart.
The music is a lure that keeps us near,
To wash away the burden of our fear.

The morning comes to stop the spinning wheel,
And leaves us standing on the cold, hard ground.
The fantasy was all too hard and real,
With nothing left but silence all around.
The carousel is still beneath the sun,
Now that the dance of midnight has been done.
