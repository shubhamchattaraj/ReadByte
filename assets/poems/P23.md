The marble pillars stand against the blue,
Where thinkers walked beneath the morning sun.
They sought the wisdom that they always knew,
Before the day of labor had begun.
The agora is humming with the crowd,
Where voices speak of glory, sharp and loud.

The scrolls are filled with words of law and might,
In ink that tells the stories of the kings.
They argue through the coolness of the night,
Of justice and the peace that virtue brings.
The city is a monument to mind,
Which leaves the dark and ignorance behind.

The olive trees are silver in the breeze,
Beside the fountain flowing clear and cold.
They sail the seas upon the wooden knees,
To claim the stories that are often told.
The history is written in the stone,
Where seeds of future empires are sown.
