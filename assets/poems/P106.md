The lighthouse here don't guide you to the shore,
it only shows you where the rocks begin,
a warning built in reverse, nothing more,
a light that says don't come, don't wander in.

I've watched it turn its beam against the sailors,
who mistake its glow for something like a friend,
but lighthouse light, when built for cautious tailors
of fate, exists to mark a certain end.

Sometimes the brightest light's a warning only,
not every glow was built to lead you home,
I've learned to read the lighthouse standing lonely,
and thank it for the danger it has shown.

So when you see a light against the water,
don't trust it blind, don't sail on faith alone,
some lighthouses were built to be the daughter
of caution, not the map that leads you home.
