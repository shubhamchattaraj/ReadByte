Up where the cold has claimed the longest nights,
a single ember burns against the dark,
it holds against the endless, freezing lights,
of stars too far to leave a warmer mark.

I've traveled north to find that stubborn glow,
a fire lit by hands long turned to frost,
it burns for reasons only it could know,
refusing every winter that it's crossed.

I sat beside it, felt its quiet heat,
against a cold that swallowed everything,
and understood that warmth does not compete,
it simply stays, no matter what winter brings.

So if you travel north and lose your way,
just look for embers burning in the white,
they'll never light the whole cold world to day,
but they'll be enough to get you through the night.
