The library is quiet as a tomb,
With rows of books that hold the breath of life.
They fill the stillness of the reading room,
And cut away the noise of human strife.
The paper smells of dust and ancient ink,
That forces all the readers now to think.

I walk between the shelves of classic lore,
And feel the presence of the writers past.
I find the things I am looking for,
In stories that were clearly made to last.
The knowledge is a treasure in the hand,
That flows across the breadth of human land.

The light is filtered through the stained glass,
And casts a color on the wooden floor.
I watch the shadows of the hours pass,
Behind the heavy, iron-bolted door.
The library is safe and it is deep,
Where all the secrets of the ages sleep.

I leave the silence of the room behind,
And step into the noise of city street.
The knowledge that I carry in my mind,
Is making all the world feel very sweet.
The library is waiting for my return,
For all the lessons that I have to learn.
