A wildflower grew inside the broken circuit,
its roots wound tight around the copper wire,
no engineer had ever meant to work it
into blueprints built for current, not for briar.

I've found it thriving where the power's silent,
a green rebellion tangled in the steel,
the wireframe wildflower, soft and quiet,
made something living out of something real.

They say machines and flowers don't mix, ever,
that nature and the circuit board oppose,
but here they've grown together, joined forever,
a stubborn, tangled, wire-and-petal rose.

So let the wildflower keep its circuit growing,
I'll let the green and copper intertwine,
some beauty comes from wires no one's knowing
still hold a garden growing, line by line.
