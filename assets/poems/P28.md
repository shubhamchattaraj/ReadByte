The city lights are bleeding in the street,
Reflecting off the pavement like a screen.
The rhythm of the night is bittersweet,
In shades of electric blue and poison green.
The rain is washing over all the grime,
And stealing back the remnants of our time.

The sirens wail a melody of chrome,
They pierce the velvet curtain of the fog.
We have no other place to call our home,
Except the circuit board and digital log.
The neon signs are flickering in tune,
Beneath the pale and synthetic moon.

The people walk like ghosts behind the glass,
With eyes that stare at nothing in the dark.
We watch the static of the present pass,
Without the chance to leave a single spark.
The rain is cold against the metal skin,
Where stories of the city all begin.

The rhapsody is ending in the hum,
Of wires singing beneath the city floor.
The future is a sound that’s yet to come,
And waiting at the threshold of the door.
The neon fades into the coming day,
And sweeps the ghosts of night so far away.
