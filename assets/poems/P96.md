The fire's mostly gone, but one light lingers,
a single ember standing in the gray,
I reach toward it with my frozen fingers,
and beg it, quiet, please don't fade away.

The night has taken everything around it,
the wood, the warmth, the voices that once sang,
but still that ember burns, as if it founded
a reason of its own to stay, to hang.

The wind will come to test if it can weather,
the cold will press to see if it will fall,
but I'll stay close and shield it, best together,
this final light against the dark and all.

So let the last ember standing keep on glowing,
I'll feed it slow with everything I've saved,
for in that single light, still burning, growing,
I've found the fire that the dark can't take.
