The city grows its trees from wire and light,
a neon orchard blooming after dark,
each sign a fruit that ripens in the night,
and drops its glow like embers in the park.

I've walked beneath the branches made of glass,
their leaves all flickering pink and blazing blue,
the sidewalk shines like rivers made of brass,
reflecting back a sky that's always new.

They say the orchard never learns to sleep,
its branches wired straight into the grid,
I've watched the neon fruit grow soft and deep,
and glow for every wish that no one did.

So walk with me beneath this wire-lit tree,
and let the colors fall like rain on skin,
the orchard blooms for anyone who's free
to wander through the night and step within.
