The firelight dances on the wall,
A phantom troupe in orange haze,
While winter waits within the hall,
To swallow up the dying blaze.
The embers sigh, the chimney sings,
Of summer fields and distant rain,
While memory on charcoal wings,
Returns to haunt the windowpane.

The logs surrender, one by one,
In crackling bursts of golden light,
The final work of summer sun,
Is given to the raven night.
A warmth lingers upon the floor,
A ghostly heat against the stone,
While tapping on the heavy door,
The wind complains in monotone.

We gather close to keep the glow,
To guard the spark from turning gray,
And watch the shadows ebb and flow,
Like ghosts of those who went away.
Each flicker is a story told,
In ash and smoke and rising heat,
Against the encroaching, biting cold,
Where past and present softly meet.

And when the last, lone coal is black,
And darkness claims the dusty room,
There is no path to lead us back,
Away from winter’s heavy loom.
The hearth is cold, the spirit thin,
The embers sleep in winter’s cage,
While silence lets the frost come in,
To turn the final, frozen page.
