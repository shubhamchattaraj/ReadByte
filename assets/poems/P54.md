The lighthouse beam is cutting through the fog,
A rhythm in the dark of oceanic deep.
I write the events in the heavy log,
While all the tired sailors are in sleep.
The ocean roars a symphony of spite,
To challenge all the comfort of the night.

I stand upon the gallery of stone,
And feel the wind that tears upon the sail.
I walk the narrow walkway all alone,
To tell the stories of the raging gale.
The light is warning all the ships to turn,
For all the lessons that we have to learn.

The dawn will come to calm the restless wave,
And leave the ocean quiet on the sand.
It guards the secrets of the lonely grave,
That lies beneath the water and the land.
The mariner is tired of the sea,
And waits for all the things that are to be.

The light is fading in the morning sun,
And leaves me with the beauty of the day.
The work of all the watch is finally done,
And sweeps the ghosts of night so far away.
The lighthouse stands in silence on the shore,
To wait until the night returns once more.
