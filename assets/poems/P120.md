The fog builds structures no one could design,
a shifting architecture, soft and gray,
its towers rise and fall without a line
of blueprint drawn to keep them, or a stay.

I've walked through hallways made of mist and vapor,
that vanish just as soon as I arrive,
the fog don't need a permit or a paper,
it builds and rules, and still it stays alive.

They say a building needs a strong foundation,
a weight to hold it steady through the years,
but fog constructs a different kind of nation,
one made of breath, and softness, and of tears.

So let the architecture keep on drifting,
I'll wander through its temporary halls,
some buildings aren't for staying, they're for lifting
the spirit briefly, then just letting fall.
