The shadows cling to corners of the room,
Like velvet curtains drawn against the light.
They weave the pattern of a quiet doom,
Within the stillness of the coming night.
They stretch across the carpet on the floor,
And wait behind the threshold of the door.

I watch them dance upon the white-washed wall,
A shape that changes with the flickering flame.
They answer to a silent, urgent call,
That whispers out the letters of my name.
The shadows have a hunger for the soul,
That makes the broken parts of spirit whole.

The darkness is a cloak that I can wear,
To hide the sorrow of the tired mind.
I breathe the coolness of the midnight air,
And leave the world of worry far behind.
The shadows are the friends I know the best,
Before I lay my weary head to rest.

The sun will rise to banish them away,
And leave the room in harsh and golden glare.
I wait until the ending of the day,
To find the peace that only they can share.
The velvet shadows call me to the deep,
Where I can find the promise of my sleep.
