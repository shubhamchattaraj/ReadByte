Between the stations lies a hidden kingdom,
ruled by hiss and crackle, gray and vast,
no king has ever claimed this strange, unwritten
domain of frequencies that flicker past.

I've traveled there by turning slow the dial,
past music, news, and voices sharp and clear,
into the static kingdom's gray denial
of anything but noise for me to hear.

The static doesn't ask you to believe it,
it doesn't promise meaning in its hum,
but sometimes, in the noise, I can perceive it,
a kingdom built from everything to come.

So if you find your dial stuck between two stations,
don't rush to turn it back to something known,
just listen to the static kingdom's patient
and gray domain, and claim it as your own.
