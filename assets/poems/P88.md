The cold don't stop at skin, it goes on further,
it finds the marrow hiding in the bone,
a kind of chill no fire dares to smother,
a cold you carry always, and alone.

I've felt it in the winters of the spirit,
not weather, but a distance, dull and slow,
it settles deep where no one else can hear it,
and waits there, patient, letting nothing show.

But even marrow warms if given time,
the deepest cold eventually recedes,
I've learned to trust the slow, uneven climb
from marrow deep to warmth that no one sees.

So if you carry cold within your bones,
don't rush the thaw, don't force the warmth to start,
some healing only comes in patient tones,
that reach, in time, the marrow of the heart.
