The fault line dances soft beneath the city,
a waltz of tension no one feels just yet,
it turns in circles, patient, without pity,
rehearsing for a partner it hasn't met.

I've felt the tremor of its quiet stepping,
a subtle shift beneath my sleeping feet,
the faultline waltz keeps time without much keeping,
of any rhythm steady or discreet.

They say a waltz needs music, needs a ballroom,
a partner dressed in silk to lead the way,
but faultlines only need the dark and small room
beneath the earth to have their private sway.

So let the faultline keep its silent dancing,
I'll build my houses knowing it exists,
some waltzes never stop their slow advancing,
they only wait for one unlucky twist.
