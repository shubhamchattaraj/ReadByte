The shelves are bowed with weight of leather books,
The dust is thick upon the wooden floor.
The shadows hide in corners and in nooks,
Behind the heavy, iron-bolted door.
The stories are as fragile as the moth,
Wrapped up within the ancient, velvet cloth.

The ink is fading on the yellow page,
A ghost of words that once were spoken loud.
It speaks of beauty from a distant age,
Above the chatter of the city crowd.
The ink is dry as bone within the well,
With nothing left of all that it could tell.

I walk among the rows of silent shelves,
And hear the whispers of the long ago.
We search for pieces of our former selves,
Within the places that the mortals know.
The archive holds the truth of human kind,
For all the ones who are still left behind.

The key is turning in the rusted lock,
To seal the secrets from the light of day.
It moves with rhythm of the ticking clock,
Before the words are turned to ash and grey.
The library is quiet in the night,
And hidden far away from human sight.
