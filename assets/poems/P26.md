The pendulum is swinging to and fro,
A heartbeat made of brass and heavy chain.
It marks the passing hours as they go,
Through cycles of the sun and pouring rain.
The ticking is a drumbeat in the wall,
That answers to a silent, urgent call.

The gears are turning in a dusty room,
Where time is measured by the falling sand.
The minutes weave a complicated loom,
Directed by a cold and steady hand.
Each second is a breath that’s taken fast,
Before it joins the shadows of the past.

The numbers fade upon the porcelain face,
A ghost of what the hour used to be.
We run our lives inside a narrow race,
And struggle for the things we cannot see.
The hands are moving onward in the light,
To chase away the coming of the night.

The final stroke is ringing in the air,
A hollow sound that lingers in the bone.
It leaves us with a sense of deep despair,
To face the ticking silence all alone.
The clock has stopped its rhythm on the shelf,
And left me here to find my inner self.
