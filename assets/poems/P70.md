The lantern swings, though nothing burns inside,
an empty glow that guides me anyway,
I've followed hollow light through low and high,
and trusted it to lead me through the gray.

Its flame went out somewhere along the road,
but still the shape remembers how to shine,
I carry it, a strange and empty load,
that lights the path like something still divine.

I've walked through nights where nothing else was true,
just swinging metal, echoing and bare,
it never asked for oil to see me through,
just faith enough to lift it through the air.

So carry on your hollow lantern too,
don't wait for flame to prove the light is real,
sometimes the emptiness is what gets you
through darkness, to whatever you can feel.
