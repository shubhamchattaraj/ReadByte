The ash begins its yearly, slow migration,
from burning field to distant, waiting ground,
it travels on the wind without a nation,
and settles where the compass can't be found.

I've watched it drift across the empty acres,
a gray and weightless flock without a wing,
the migration of ash makes no forsakers,
it goes wherever quiet breezes bring.

They say migrations follow ancient patterns,
a memory encoded deep in bone,
but ash migrates by nothing that much matters,
just wind, and chance, and wherever it's blown.

So let it drift, this yearly gray migration,
I'll watch it settle soft on foreign land,
and know that even ash needs a vacation,
from the fire that it never chose, or planned.
