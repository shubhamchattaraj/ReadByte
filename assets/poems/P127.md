The feathers fell before the sky collapsed,
a warning written light in white and gray,
the birds knew something none of us had grasped,
and left before the ending had its say.

I've kept a feather from that quiet warning,
a weightless proof that something soft can know,
the apocalypse arrived without a mourning,
just feathers drifting down before the blow.

They say the end should come with fire and thunder,
a violent, loud, undoing of the skies,
but mine was soft, a feather torn asunder
from wings that chose to leave before the cries.

So keep the feather light against the ending,
a featherweight against the heavy fall,
some doom arrives so gently, unoffending,
you'd miss it if you weren't watching at all.
