There's a station broadcasting after midnight,
a nightshade frequency both sweet and bitter,
it plays for those still lingering past twilight,
its purple static neither loud nor quieter.

I've tuned in on the nights I couldn't settle,
and heard the nightshade humming through the dark,
it doesn't fix you, doesn't test your mettle,
it only keeps you company past dark.

They say the plant that shares this station's title,
can heal or poison, careful with the dose,
the nightshade radio remains as vital,
a comfort that stays dangerous up close.

So keep it low, this softly purple station,
I'll listen till the nightshade hour ends,
some comfort comes with careful moderation,
a frequency that's neither foes nor friends.
