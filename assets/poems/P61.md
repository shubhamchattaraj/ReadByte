The embers drift where rivers used to burn,
and ash falls soft like snow that lost its name,
each spark a word the fire failed to learn,
each wave of light no ocean dares to claim.

I walked the shore when smoke was still a tide,
and watched the coals go rolling out to sea,
the night pulled close, and nothing left to hide,
just glowing salt and what remains of me.

No flame outlives the water's patient pull,
no ember holds its color long enough,
yet still I stand and let the dark be full
of light that dies but never turns to rough.

So call it tide, or call it dying fire,
the name won't change the way it leaves my chest,
I'll keep on walking toward the edge, and higher,
until the ember tide has laid to rest.
