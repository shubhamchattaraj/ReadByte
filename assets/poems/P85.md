The old machines have learned to dream in rust,
their gears gone still, their pistons quiet, slow,
they sit in fields, surrendered now to dust,
remembering the engines they used to know.

I walk among them, run my hand along
the flaking orange of forgotten steel,
and swear I hear the echo of a song,
some engine hum that only rust can feel.

I think of all the roads that these machines
once carried, all the weight they used to bear,
and wonder what a rusted engine means,
when dreaming's all that's left for it to share.

So let them rust, let reverie take hold,
there's dignity in resting after use,
these old machines, now quiet, tired, and old,
still dream of roads that rust cannot erase.
