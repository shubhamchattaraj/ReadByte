The city sleeps in amber light,
Beneath a dome of polished rain,
Where stars are swallowed by the night,
And silence flows through every vein.
We watch the world from under glass,
As seasons turn in shades of gray,
And wait for ghosts of time to pass,
Before the birth of another day.

The streets are veins of glowing neon,
Where taxis hum like distant bees,
A lonely, electric, cold aeon,
Among the synthetic, silent trees.
We press our palms against the pane,
To feel the chill of falling sky,
To hide the grief and mask the pain,
As all the hurried lives go by.

No touch is real, no word is heard,
Just echoes trapped in crystal walls,
Where every heavy, spoken word,
Into the empty distance falls.
We live as specimens on display,
In jars of time and rings of lead,
While slowly turning into gray,
With nothing left to do or said.

But look—a crack begins to crawl,
A jagged line of sudden hope,
To shatter down the inner wall,
And cut the thin, synthetic rope.
The glass gives way to rushing air,
The city sounds begin to roar,
And finally, we truly dare,
To step beyond the closing door.
