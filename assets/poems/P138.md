The smokestack hums a low, industrial serenade,
a courtship song of steam and rust and heat,
it plays for no one, but it's never stayed
its steady rhythm, tireless and complete.

I've worked beneath its towering, gray shadow,
and learned to love the serenade it plays,
a song for every factory and meadow
that traded green for steel in modern days.

They say a serenade should woo a lover,
should soften hearts with strings and gentle tone,
but smokestacks only have themselves to smother
the silence of the world they've come to own.

So let the smokestack hum its steady verses,
industrial and honest, rough and true,
some serenades are only steam that pierces
the quiet with a song both old and new.
