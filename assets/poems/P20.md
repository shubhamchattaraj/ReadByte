The ocean is a beast with restless waves,
That crash against the hull of rotting wood.
It hides the sailors in their watery graves,
Where legends say they once have bravely stood.
The salt is thick upon the weary skin,
Where endless tides and currents will begin.

The compass spins without a steady hand,
Beneath the heavy clouds of slate and grey.
We dream of solid earth and dry, warm land,
But sail upon the mist of yesterday.
The albatross is circling above,
To watch the ship and what it’s made of.

The anchor drops into the deep abyss,
To hold us steady through the coming storm.
We trade the shore for such a dark, cold kiss,
Where silence keeps the broken spirits warm.
The sea is calling names we used to know,
Before we started sailing long ago.
