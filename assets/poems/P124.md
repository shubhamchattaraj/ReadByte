I am the quiet arsonist of habits,
I burn the patterns that no longer serve,
I strike the match on comfort like a rabbit
too slow to run from what it doesn't deserve.

I've watched old versions of myself go smoking,
their ashes settling on familiar ground,
the quiet arsonist isn't for provoking,
just clearing space for something to be found.

They say that fire's always meant for ruin,
that burning only ever means the end,
but I have learned that careful, quiet doing
of burning old things helps the new to mend.

So let the quiet arsonist keep striking,
small matches held to habits grown too tight,
some burning's just the soul's own quiet liking
for starting over, gentle, in the light.
