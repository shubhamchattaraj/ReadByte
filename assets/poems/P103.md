I hung a tinfoil sky above my bedroom,
a constellation cut from kitchen scraps,
it caught the lamp and scattered through the dim room
a thousand crooked, homemade starlit maps.

It wasn't real, but neither were my wishes,
the ones I made on stars I'd never seen,
just paper, foil, and folded metal dishes,
reflecting back a hope that felt between.

The real stars burn too far to hold or borrow,
but tinfoil ones will bend to how I feel,
I built a sky to hold my every sorrow,
in crumpled light that's honest, if not real.

So let the tinfoil constellation glitter,
above the bed where all my wishing starts,
a homemade sky is never somehow littler,
than one that's real, if it still holds our hearts.
