The quill is scratching on the parchment sheet,
A record of the voices from the past.
It captures all the rhythm and the beat,
Of stories that were surely made to last.
The ink is black as night within the well,
With all the things that I am meant to tell.

I listen to the wind against the wall,
And write the whispers that it leaves behind.
I answer to a distant, urgent call,
Of all the wisdom that I have to find.
The scroll is filling with the heavy lines,
To trace the logic of the grand designs.

The scribe is sitting in the candle glow,
And working until morning starts to rise.
He knows the things that mortals ought to know,
And sees the world with all its clever lies.
The parchment is a treasure that I keep,
Within the library of final sleep.

The morning sun will wash the words away,
And leave the paper empty and so white.
I wait until the ending of the day,
To write the secrets of the velvet night.
The scribe is finished with the duty now,
To fulfill all the pieces of the vow.
