The heavy curtains cling to rotting frames,
Where dust motes spin in shafts of sickly grey.
The portrait whispers long-forgotten names,
And begs the sun to never start the day.
The iron gates are rusted shut with grief,
Providing us with little true relief.

There is a coldness creeping down the hall,
Like fingers tracing patterns on the wall.
The portraits turn their faces from the call,
As shadows rise and start to slowly crawl.
The candles sputter once and then expire,
Beneath the weight of some infernal fire.

I hear the clock strike thirteen in the dark,
A hollow sound that echoes through the bone.
It leaves upon my heart a jagged mark,
A symphony played by the wind alone.
The ghosts are stirring in the velvet gloom,
Within the confines of this ancient tomb.
