The lighthouse stands against the raging gale,
A pillar made of stone and hardened grit.
We watch the ships that lower every sail,
Beneath the warning lamp that has been lit.
The ocean roars a symphony of spite,
To challenge all the comfort of the night.

I pace the narrow walkway of the tower,
And feel the structure shiver in the storm.
I know that I am losing every power,
To keep the fragile heart within me warm.
The iron door is rattled by the sea,
Which calls for all the things that used to be.

The beam of light is cutting through the haze,
A rhythmic pulse that guides the sailors home.
It traces out a bright and steady maze,
Above the cresting peaks of white and foam.
I count the seconds passing in the dark,
And wait for just a single sign or spark.

The dawn will come to calm the restless deep,
And leave the ocean quiet on the sand.
The secrets that the silent waters keep,
Are hidden far from reach of any hand.
The lighthouse waits until the day is done,
To trade the starlight for the rising sun.
