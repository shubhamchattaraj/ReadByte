The tapestry is woven in the dark,
A fabric made of history and gold.
It captures every single human spark,
And every secret that has been told.
The threads are intricate and very fine,
To trace the logic of the grand design.

I see the figures dancing on the cloth,
A frozen moment of the long ago.
They flicker like the wings of summer moth,
In rhythms that the ancient people know.
The tapestry is fragile in the light,
And needs the shadows of the velvet night.

The Weaver works the loom with steady hand,
To pull the threads together in a seam.
It covers all the breadth of human land,
And builds the structure of a living dream.
The tapestry is finished on the wall,
To witness all the rise and final fall.

I stand and study every single knot,
And try to find the meaning of the thread.
The things that time has surely long forgot,
Are waking in the spirits of the dead.
The tapestry is constant and is true,
To change the world for every life anew.
