The tinbell rings a prophecy of weather,
a hollow chime that knows before the storm,
it hangs above the door, light as a feather,
and warns the house of changes taking form.

I've learned to trust its trembling, small predictions,
before the clouds have even learned to gray,
the tinbell doesn't deal in contradictions,
it only rings the truth of coming day.

They say a prophet needs a voice for shouting,
a vision grand enough to make you kneel,
but mine's a bell, soft-spoken, never doubting,
that quiet warnings are still just as real.

So let the tinbell keep its gentle ringing,
I'll listen closer than the loudest sign,
some prophecies are only small bells singing
the weather changes, one soft chime at a time.
