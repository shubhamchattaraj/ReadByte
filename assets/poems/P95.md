I carry no map, only a compass worn,
its needle pointing not to north, but on,
I've learned to trust the wandering, unsworn
to any place until the day is gone.

The desert doesn't ask me for a reason,
the mountains never question why I roam,
I've walked through every kind of shifting season,
without the weight of ever calling home.

My compass doesn't point to any city,
it only points to motion, steady, true,
and though the wandering can feel like pity,
it's just the only way I've learned to move through.

So let me keep my nomad's compass turning,
not toward a place, but toward the next unknown,
there's something in the wandering, the yearning,
that feels, at last, the closest thing to home.
