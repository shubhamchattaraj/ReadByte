The desert heat is rising from the sand,
A wave of shimmer in the midday glare.
It spreads across the breadth of barren land,
With nothing but the silence in the air.
The cactus stands as witness to the sun,
Until the day of burning heat is done.

A hawk is circling in the azure height,
With shadow cast upon the ground below.
It searches for the prey in fading light,
Where all the desert rivers used to flow.
The wind is dry against the parched skin,
Where all the tales of emptiness begin.

I walk the track that leads to nowhere fast,
And watch the dust rise up behind my feet.
The memories of all the things that passed,
Are scattered in the middle of the heat.
The horizon is a promise of the blue,
That never seems to ever come so true.

The stars will rule the cooling of the night,
Above the dunes that shift into the dark.
They offer up a cold and distant light,
Without the feeling of a human spark.
The desert is a kingdom built of pride,
Where secrets of the earth have long to hide.
