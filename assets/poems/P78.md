The line that split the world in two is cracked,
a broken meridian across the map,
no longer straight, no longer neatly stacked,
just fractured edges caught in time's own trap.

I've stood on both sides of the broken line,
uncertain which direction calls me home,
the east and west no longer clean align,
they bleed together where I choose to roam.

I walk the fracture, feel it shift beneath,
the ground uncertain who it should belong,
but in the breaking there's a kind of wreath,
of freedom found in lines that don't stay strong.

So let the meridian stay cracked and torn,
I'll make my compass out of broken parts,
a truer map than any ever born
from straight lines drawn by cold, unbending hearts.
