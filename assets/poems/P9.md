The floorboards moan beneath a weightless tread,
Though every door is locked against the night.
A frantic pulse hums underneath the bed,
And shadows stretch beyond the reach of light.
Something is breathing, rhythmic, cold, and slow,
Tracing the patterns that the cobwebs grow.

You find a mirror cracked in jagged lines,
But see a face that isn't quite your own.
The clockwork heart within the ceiling pines,
A ticking secret carved in marrow-bone.
The walls are thinning, turning into glass,
Watching the formless things that softly pass.

A heavy hand descends upon your neck,
Yet when you turn, the room is stark and bare.
The house is rotting, turning into wreck,
With voices tangled in the stale, dead air.
You realize then, as sanity gives way,
You are the ghost that chose the house to stay.
