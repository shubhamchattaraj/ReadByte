The sun wakes up with a golden flare,
To chase the mist from the morning air.
The dew-drops cling to the velvet leaf,
A tiny life, so bright and brief.

The river hums a low-tuned song,
It winds its way where the reeds belong.
Over the pebbles, smooth and gray,
It dances on through the heat of day.

The forest sighs in a rhythmic sway,
As shadows stretch at the end of play.
The crickets chime in the velvet night,
Beneath the moon, so cold and white.

From mountain peak to the ocean floor,
The pulse of earth beats evermore.
In every gust and in every seed,
Nature fulfills its ancient creed.
