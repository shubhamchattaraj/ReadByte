The garden is a maze of blooming rose,
A riot of the color and the scent.
It captures all the beauty that it knows,
And every moment that the sun has lent.
The bees are humming in the summer air,
To find the nectar that is hidden there.

I walk the path between the rows of green,
And feel the soft and delicate embrace.
The garden is the loveliest I’ve seen,
Within the confines of this quiet place.
The petals fall like velvet on the ground,
Without a single whisper or a sound.

The fountain in the middle of the square,
Is playing music for the silent trees.
It cools the heavy and the humid air,
And dances in the rhythm of the breeze.
The garden is a world of gentle grace,
That lights the smile upon a weary face.

The autumn comes to change the shade of gold,
And strip the garden of its vibrant dress.
The stories that the summer often told,
Are left within a state of quietness.
The garden waits for spring to come anew,
To bathe the world in all its morning dew.
