The flowers bloom in shades of neon light,
A harvest grown from metal and from dust.
They glow within the belly of the night,
And thrive within the iron and the rust.
The alchemist is tending to the bed,
To wake the spirits of the long since dead.

I mix the liquid in a crystal bowl,
And feed the roots of every silver vine.
It satisfies the hunger of the soul,
And makes the garden seem truly divine.
The petals shimmer with a metallic sheen,
The strangest garden that I’ve ever seen.

The bees are humming with a heavy drone,
And collecting nectar from the poisoned well.
The garden is a world that I have known,
With all the secrets that I have to tell.
The alchemist is proud of what he made,
Before the colors start to slowly fade.

The morning light will banish all the glow,
And leave the garden quiet in the sun.
I watch the way the living spirits grow,
Now that the work of alchemy is done.
The alchemist is resting in the shade,
Within the garden that his hands have made.
