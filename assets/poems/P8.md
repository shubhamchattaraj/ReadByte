The starship drifts through veils of violet dust,
Where ancient dragons coil round solar flares,
With scales of chrome and hearts of tempered rust,
They breathe the neon breath of dying airs.
The cockpit glows with runes of shifting light,
As hyper-drives entwine with wizard's flight.

Below, a planet forged of glass and dream,
Where knight-bots joust upon a gravity tide,
Their laser-swords ignite a molten stream,
While ghosts of forgotten gods within them hide.
The iron forest hums a digital psalm,
Beneath a synthetic sky, profoundly calm.

Then silence falls as time begins to bend,
The quantum gates unlock with silver keys,
Where myth and math pursue a sudden end,
Lost in the depths of starlit, dreaming seas.
The cosmos sighs, a spell cast long ago,
In circuits deep, where spirit-waters flow.
