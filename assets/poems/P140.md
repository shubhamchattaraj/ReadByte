The rustbelt sings its hymnal to the closing,
a chorus made of silent factory bells,
the workers hum a tune of quiet, dozing
in towns where industry no longer dwells.

I've heard the hymnal echo through the alleys,
a requiem for engines gone to sleep,
the rustbelt hymnal never counts or tallies,
it only sings the promises to keep.

They say a hymnal needs a church for singing,
a congregation gathered, robed in white,
but rustbelt hymns are only quietly ringing
through empty mills that used to burn all night.

So let the rustbelt keep its hymnal playing,
a song for those who built and stayed behind,
some hymns are only meant for slow delaying
the silence of a town that time unwind.
