The ledger of ash keeps every burning tally,
in columns written soft with smoke and char,
it counts what's lost in every wooded valley,
and marks the distance embers learned to travel too far.

I've balanced my own losses in its pages,
against the growth that always follows fire,
the ledger doesn't judge across the ages,
it simply counts what burned, and what stayed higher.

They say the books should always end in profit,
a sum that proves the burning was worthwhile,
but ash accounts don't work that way, don't scoff it,
they measure grief in every careful pile.

So keep the ledger open, keep it honest,
let every burned and rebuilt thing be filed,
the ash remembers, patient and unpromised,
the cost of every fire, gentle or wild.
