She plucks the stars from velvet skies,
To spin a thread of shimmering blue,
With starlight trapped within her eyes,
She weaves the dreams that once were true.
A loom of wind, a shuttle fast,
She binds the chaos into form,
To make the fragile shadows last,
Before the coming of the storm.

The warp is night, the woof is day,
A crisscross pattern, tight and thin,
To keep the wandering ghosts away,
And let the dawn of morning in.
She hums a melody of old,
Of tangled webs and silver lace,
While stories of the brave and bold,
Are woven in the hollow space.

Each knot is heavy with a name,
Each stitch is soaked in human salt,
She feeds the tapestry the flame,
To hide the tear, to hide the fault.
It grows beneath her clever hands,
A map of places never seen,
Across the reach of shifting sands,
And fields of never-ending green.

But as the weaver reaches end,
The fabric starts to fray and tear,
With nothing left for her to mend,
In all the empty, biting air.
She lets the needle drop to rest,
And watches as the colors fade,
The fading beat within her chest,
Is silenced by the light she made.
