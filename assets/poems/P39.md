The forest floor is carpeted in pine,
A scent of earth that lingers on the breeze.
The shadows of the branches start to twine,
Beneath the canopy of ancient trees.
The sunlight filters in a gentle stream,
To build the structure of a living dream.

I walk among the roots of elder oak,
And feel the presence of the hidden things.
The forest wears a heavy velvet cloak,
That hides the flutter of the dragon wings.
The silence is a language that I know,
Where all the seeds of ancient wisdom grow.

A deer is watching from the leafy screen,
With eyes that hold the color of the moss.
The woodland world is vibrant and is green,
Without a sense of tragedy or loss.
I sit beneath the boughs and start to pray,
Before the ending of the final day.

The forest breathes in rhythm with my heart,
A cycle that is steady and is true.
I know that we will never be apart,
Within the world that always feels so new.
The forest is a sanctuary kept,
Where all the gods of nature long have slept.
