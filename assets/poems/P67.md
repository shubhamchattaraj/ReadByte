I lost the needle somewhere in the dark,
a compass spinning slow with nothing true,
no north to follow, not a single mark,
just quiet turning underneath the blue.

The old maps folded soft against my chest,
their ink gone faded, rivers drawn in doubt,
I kept on walking toward the furthest west,
and let the silence tell me where I'm out.

The stars won't always shine when I look up,
the clouds will hide the answers that I seek,
but something in me fills the empty cup,
and points me forward though the needle's weak.

So let it spin, let silence be the map,
I'll find my way by feeling, not by sight,
the compass sleeps but I don't need the map,
to know that I am walking toward the light.
