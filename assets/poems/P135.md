They say a feather weighs almost to nothing,
a gram of keratin and hollow bone,
but I have felt the weight of feathers, something
far heavier than what the scale has shown.

I've carried one for years inside my pocket,
a small reminder of a bird now gone,
its weightlessness became a kind of locket,
that somehow holds more than I lean upon.

They measure weight in numbers, cold and certain,
in units clean and separate from the heart,
but grief will always lift the heavy curtain,
on how light objects carry weightier parts.

So let the feather stay however weightless,
I'll feel its heaviness the way I choose,
some burdens don't show up on scales, and greatness
of weight is felt in what we cannot lose.
