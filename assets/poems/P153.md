My compass turned to ash somewhere in walking,
its needle burned before it found the way,
but even ashen tools are somehow talking,
in quiet, gray, directional decay.

I've followed what the compass left behind me,
a trail of soot instead of solid north,
the ashen compass doesn't seem to bind me
to any single, well-established course.

They say you need a compass sharp and steady,
unburned, and true, and pointing straight ahead,
but mine's in ashes, and I'm still already
finding my way by what the burning said.

So let the compass stay however ashen,
I'll trust the gray more than I trust the gold,
some guidance comes from embers, not from fashion
of needles built for stories rarely told.
