The wires hum a song across the plains,
A message sent through dark and hollow air.
It speaks of all the losses and the gains,
Of all the burdens that we have to share.
The clicking key is rhythmic in the room,
To drive away the shadow and the gloom.

I tap the code that travels through the night,
A series of the dots and dashes long.
It guides the ships with flicker of the light,
And keeps the spirit steady and so strong.
The ink is dry upon the paper tape,
To give the hidden words a solid shape.

The operator listens to the sound,
And writes the meaning in a ledger book.
I keep the truth that I have surely found,
Within the quiet of the lonely nook.
The telegraph is singing to the soul,
To make the broken parts of spirit whole.

The morning light will silence every key,
And leave the wires empty in the sun.
The world is wide and waiting for to see,
Now that the work of midnight has been done.
The messages are drifting in the haze,
To guide the people through the coming days.
