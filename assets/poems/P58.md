The shelves are filled with books that have no words,
A library of silence and of grey.
I watch the flying of the phantom birds,
As they go drifting on their lonely way.
The librarian is living in the dark,
And keeping hold of every dying spark.

I find the stories in the empty air,
And read the history upon the wall.
I find the things that I am meant to share,
Before I answer to the final call.
The shadows are the characters I know,
From all the places that I used to go.

I dust the covers of the ancient tomes,
And organize the history of the mind.
I build the shelter for the wandering homes,
For all the people that are left behind.
The librarian is happy in the gloom,
Within the quiet of the reading room.

The morning sun will cast a golden ray,
And make the shadows vanish in the light.
I wait until the ending of the day,
To return to the comfort of the night.
The librarian is sleeping with the past,
In stories that were clearly made to last.
