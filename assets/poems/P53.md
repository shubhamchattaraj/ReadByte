The trees are heavy with the fruit of years,
Each apple is a memory of old.
It holds the laughter and it holds the tears,
And stories that were never truly told.
The orchard stands against the morning light,
Before the coming of the velvet night.

I harvest all the sweetness of the time,
And make a cider from the golden pressed.
It tastes of all the beauty and the rhyme,
That once had put my weary heart to rest.
The orchard is a blessing to the soul,
That makes the broken parts of spirit whole.

The blossoms fall like snow upon the ground,
To signify the passing of the day.
I listen to the silence all around,
And watch the seasons as they drift away.
The orchard is a sanctuary kept,
Where all the gods of nature long have slept.

The winter wind will strip the branches bare,
And leave the orchard waiting in the cold.
I breathe the coolness of the winter air,
To watch the stories of the world unfold.
The orchard waits for spring to come again,
To heal the heart of all the mortal pain.
