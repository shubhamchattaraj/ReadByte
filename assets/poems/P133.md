Between the ember and the frost line, standing,
I feel the pull of both the heat and cold,
one hand toward the fire, understanding,
the other toward the winter, growing old.

I've never learned to choose between the seasons,
to pick the burning over frozen ground,
perhaps there's not supposed to be a reason,
just balance where the opposites are found.

They say a heart can't hold both fire and frost line,
that warmth and cold cannot occupy the same,
but mine has drawn a soft, uncertain cost line,
between the ember and the winter's name.

So let me stand between the frost and burning,
neither fully warmed nor fully cold,
some hearts are only comfortable in learning
to hold the opposite truths they're told.
