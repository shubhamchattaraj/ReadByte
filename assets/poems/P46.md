The mirror is a portal to the past,
A silver surface reflecting all the fear.
The moments that we know can never last,
Are captured in the glass and become clear.
The light is distorted in the jagged frame,
Which hides the shadow of a forgotten name.

I stare into the eyes of someone else,
A stranger with the look of long ago.
I try to save the broken parts of shelves,
Within the places that the mortals know.
The reflection is a ghost within the room,
That dances in the shadow of the gloom.

The glass is cold against the finger tip,
And sends a shiver down the weary spine.
I feel the world of logic slowly slip,
And cross the borders of the solid line.
The mirror shows the truth I try to hide,
With nowhere left for anyone to bide.

The day will break to banish all the dark,
And leave the mirror silent on the wall.
It waits for just another human spark,
To answer to a final, ghostly call.
The reflection fades into the empty grey,
And sweeps the ghosts of night so far away.
