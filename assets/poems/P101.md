A requiem in cobalt fills the evening,
blue like a bruise the sky forgot to hide,
I hum along, my quiet heart believing
the color knows the words I've never tried.

It plays for every ship that's lost its harbor,
for every star too far to send its light,
the cobalt requiem, both plain and larger
than any grief I've named or held tonight.

I don't know who composed this blue lament,
or why it chose the hour just past four,
but something in its quiet is well spent,
on all the things worth grieving, and much more.

So let the cobalt requiem keep playing,
I'll sit beneath its slow and steady blue,
and trust that grief, like evening, isn't staying,
it only comes to carry something through.
