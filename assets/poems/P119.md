I built a strange contraption made for winter,
of gears and pipes that hum against the cold,
it doesn't heat the room, it's just a hinter
of warmth that lives in stories left untold.

I wind it up each evening before sleeping,
and listen to its clicking, patient song,
the midwinter contraption keeps on keeping
a rhythm through the nights that feel too long.

They say a machine should serve a purpose,
should heat, or light, or move you toward a goal,
but mine exists just so the winter's surface
has something ticking near the frozen soul.

So let the gears keep turning through December,
I'll wind the strange contraption every night,
some machines exist just to remember
that winter's dark still needs a little light.
