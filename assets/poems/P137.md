I built a kingdom out of sea-worn glass,
each piece a jewel polished by the tide,
the driftglass throne is humble, but I pass
my days as ruler with a quiet pride.

The waves deliver tribute every morning,
green, brown, and blue, worn smooth by salt and time,
I've never needed trumpets or a warning,
just glass, and patience, rendered into rhyme.

They say a kingdom needs an army standing,
a wall of stone to keep the danger out,
but mine is only glass, softly demanding
nothing but the tide to bring about.

So let the driftglass kingdom keep on growing,
one polished piece collected from the shore,
some thrones are only built from patient knowing
that softness rules as well as any war.
