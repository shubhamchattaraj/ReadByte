My compass rose has petals bent and broken,
each direction pointing slightly off from true,
but even broken tools have somehow spoken
a kind of honest map I still pursue.

I've followed norths that led me toward the ocean,
and easts that curved into a setting sun,
the broken compass rose, in constant motion,
has taught me more than any perfect one.

They say you need a needle straight and steady,
to find your way through unfamiliar land,
but I have learned that broken makes you ready
to trust the walking more than trust your hand.

So let my compass rose stay bent and crooked,
I'll follow wherever crooked wants to go,
some paths were never meant to be rebooked
by tools too straight to let their wildness show.
