I've lost my compass in a fog of burning,
its needle spinning slow through drifting smoke,
the compass of smoke keeps softly turning,
toward directions no one's ever spoke.

I follow it through streets I can't remember,
led on by scent instead of steady sight,
the compass doesn't care if it's December,
it only points toward warmth, or maybe light.

They say a compass needs a north that's certain,
a fixed direction pulling every time,
but mine's a compass built behind the curtain
of smoke, uncertain, drifting, hard to find.

So let me follow smoke instead of magnets,
and trust the drifting more than trust the pole,
some paths are only found by scent and hazard,
directions written soft into the soul.
