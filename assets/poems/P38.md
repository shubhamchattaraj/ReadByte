The clockwork bird is singing on the wire,
With feathers made of brass and silver thread.
It burns with cold and unconsuming fire,
And wakes the sleeping spirits of the dead.
Its melody is sharp and metallic sweet,
Above the bustle of the crowded street.

The gears are ticking in a hollow chest,
A rhythm that is precise and very cold.
The bird will never take a moment's rest,
To watch the stories of the world unfold.
It sees the people passing in the rain,
Without a hint of sorrow or of pain.

A spring is wound by hand of unseen ghost,
To keep the song alive within the air.
It sings to everyone and to the host,
Of all the things that we are meant to share.
The notes are drifting in the heavy smog,
Across the memory of the digital log.

The bird will stop its singing in the end,
When gears are worn and metal starts to fray.
The broken parts will never truly mend,
Before the morning light of breaking day.
The clockwork bird is silent on the wall,
To wait until the final bugle call.
