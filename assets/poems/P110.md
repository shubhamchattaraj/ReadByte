The tide came in and gave the shore an alibi,
for every footprint washed before the dawn,
it swore the sand was empty, told no lie
about the secrets that the night had drawn.

I've used the water's story as my cover,
let waves erase the evidence of me,
the tidewater alibi, my quiet lover,
that keeps my wandering hidden from the sea.

They say the ocean never learns to whisper,
that every tide reports what it has seen,
but mine has kept its silence, like a sister
who covers for the places I have been.

So let the tidewater alibi keep guarding
the secrets I have buried in the sand,
some truths are only safe when tides are warding
the footprints of a story no one planned.
